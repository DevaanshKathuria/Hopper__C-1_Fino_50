# Problem Statement

## Sector Context

Indian equity markets generate large volumes of daily stock-level trading data across multiple sectors. For investors, analysts, and student researchers, this creates an opportunity to compare sector behavior, stock performance, and trading quality over time using reproducible analytics.

## Business Problem

Indian investors and portfolio analysts need to identify which NIFTY-related stocks and sectors provide the best balance of returns, volatility, liquidity, delivery strength, and trading reliability. Raw market data alone is difficult to interpret without structured cleaning, analysis, and visualization.

## Decision-Maker Persona

The primary decision-maker is a market analyst or retail-investor-focused portfolio reviewer who wants evidence-based comparisons across stocks and sectors before making shortlist, allocation, or monitoring decisions.

## Project Objectives

- Organize and preserve raw stock market data in a reproducible repository structure.
- Build Python-based ETL steps for cleaning and standardizing historical market records.
- Explore stock- and sector-level trends through EDA and descriptive KPIs.
- Quantify risk and trading characteristics using statistical analysis.
- Deliver Tableau-ready outputs for dashboards and presentation.
- Produce academic documentation suitable for review, demo, and viva.

## Scope

- NIFTY-50-related historical stock market records from the selected Kaggle dataset
- Cleaning and documentation of raw row-level data
- Sector mapping through metadata where available
- Stock and sector comparisons using performance, volatility, liquidity, and delivery indicators
- Python analysis and Tableau-ready outputs
- Final report, screenshots, and submission preparation

## Out of Scope

- Live trading or real-time market execution systems
- Investment advice or guaranteed return recommendations
- External proprietary data sources that cannot be reproduced by the team
- Complex forecasting claims unless they are explicitly implemented, validated, and documented

## Success Criteria

- The repository structure is clean, understandable, and reproducible.
- Raw, processed, and output data locations are clearly separated.
- Documentation explains datasets, business context, and responsibilities.
- ETL, EDA, and statistical analysis can be rerun from the repository.
- Tableau-ready outputs are available for dashboard development.
- Final deliverables are clear enough for academic assessment and viva discussion.
